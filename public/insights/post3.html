The Critical Role of AEDs in Public Spaces

MAR 26, 2026

Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) are lifesavers. This article explores the importance of deploying AEDs in communities and workplaces, and provides a step-by-step guide on how to use one in an emergency. Early defibrillation is key to surviving sudden cardiac arrest.

Why Public Access to AEDs Matters

Sudden cardiac arrest (SCA) can happen to anyone, anywhere. When it strikes, every minute counts. The chance of survival decreases by about 10% for every minute that passes without defibrillation. Placing AEDs in public places like offices, schools, airports, and shopping centers drastically shortens the time to first shock, bridging the gap until emergency medical services arrive.

Using an AED: A Simple Guide

Modern AEDs are designed for use by laypersons. They provide clear, calm voice prompts to guide the user through the entire process. The basic steps are: 1) Turn on the AED. 2) Attach the electrode pads to the patient's bare chest as shown in the diagrams. 3) Allow the AED to analyze the patient's heart rhythm. 4) If a shock is advised, ensure no one is touching the patient and press the shock button. It's that simple.
