DIN 13164:2022 Compliance: Regulatory Risks and Requirements for EU Vehicle First Aid Kits

MAY 15, 2026

The enforcement of the DIN 13164:2022 standard is no longer optional for the European market. As member states begin active road safety inspections, distributors must ensure their inventory meets the updated technical requirements to avoid significant operational disruptions.

The Financial and Legal Risks of Non-Compliance

Failure to supply or carry kits that meet the 2022 standard—which now mandates the inclusion of medical face masks (Type IIR or FFP2)—results in immediate exposure to:

  • Direct Penalties: In countries like Greece and Germany, roadside inspections are resulting in €30 fines for missing, incomplete, or expired vehicle kits.
  • Customs Obstacles: European customs authorities are prioritizing the inspection of medical goods. Kits lacking the CE MDR mark or the correct DIN 13164:2022 bill of materials are liable for seizure and rejection at the port of entry.
  • Inventory Depreciation: Many standard kits offer only a 2-3 year shelf life. We provide a validated 5-year expiration date, significantly reducing the risk of dead stock and increasing the replacement interval for your end-users.

Technical Specifications & Verification

Our vehicle first aid kits are fully compliant with the latest EU standards:

  • Certified: CE MDR (TÜV SÜD) and ISO 13485.
  • Standard: Full DIN 13164:2022 component list (including mandatory masks).
  • Durability: Available in both space-saving soft bags and impact-resistant plastic cases.
