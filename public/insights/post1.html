How to Choose the Right Stretcher for Emergency Medical Services

JAN 20, 2026

Selecting the appropriate stretcher is crucial for patient safety and rescuer efficiency. This guide covers key factors to consider, from material and weight capacity to folding mechanisms and specialized features for different scenarios. Making an informed choice can significantly impact outcomes in critical situations.

1. Assess the Environment

Consider where the stretcher will be used most. Urban environments might favor compact, easily maneuverable stretchers, while rural or wilderness rescue demands lightweight, durable, and often foldable models. For industrial sites, look for high-strength materials resistant to chemicals or extreme temperatures.

2. Understand Patient Needs

The type of patient and injury dictates the choice. A scoop stretcher is ideal for suspected spinal injuries as it minimizes patient movement. A foldable stretcher is versatile for general transport, while a spine board is essential for full immobilization. Consider bariatric models for heavier patients to ensure safety and dignity.

3. Evaluate Material and Durability

Common materials include aluminum alloy, carbon fiber, and high-density polyethylene (HDPE). Aluminum offers a good balance of strength and weight. Carbon fiber is exceptionally lightweight and strong but comes at a higher cost. HDPE is durable, easy to clean, and X-ray translucent, making it perfect for spine boards.
