Small Accessories, Bigger Orders: Why Outdoor Brands Are Adding First Aid Kits

JUL 17, 2026

Outdoor brands have spent years perfecting their core products.

Better tents.

Lighter rooftop tents.

Stronger camping furniture.

Smarter storage systems.

But many brands are discovering that growth doesn't always come from launching another flagship product.

Sometimes, it comes from adding one small accessory.

Customers Rarely Buy Just One Thing

Think about a typical outdoor customer.

They come looking for a rooftop tent.

Before checkout, they also need:

  • Camping lights
  • Recovery gear
  • Cooking equipment
  • Water storage
  • Emergency essentials

Every additional product that naturally fits the trip increases the total order value.

The question is simple:

Will customers need to visit another website after shopping with you?

Safety Is Becoming Part of the Outdoor Experience

Modern outdoor customers aren't only thinking about comfort.

They're thinking about preparation.

Whether it's a weekend camping trip or a remote overlanding adventure, basic safety equipment has become part of the packing list.

That's why more outdoor brands are adding compact first aid kits alongside their traditional camping accessories.

Not because they want to become medical suppliers.

Because safety now belongs in the outdoor lifestyle.

Keep the Choice Simple

One mistake many companies make is offering too many options.

Instead, a simple three-level range makes product selection easy.

MINI

Ideal for day hikes, picnics and casual camping.

A compact, easy add-on purchase.

STANDARD

Designed for family camping, caravans and weekend adventures.

A natural companion to most camping equipment.

PLUS

Built for overlanding, remote travel and longer expeditions where customers expect more comprehensive preparation.

Three products.

Three customer types.

No unnecessary complexity.

Completing the Basket

For outdoor brands, first aid kits don't replace existing products.

They complete them.

A customer buying a rooftop tent isn't changing their purchase decision.

They're simply making their adventure more complete.

And for retailers, that often means:

  • Higher basket value
  • More complete product collections
  • Better customer satisfaction
  • More reasons for customers to return

Sometimes, business growth isn't about selling something different.

It's about selling one more thing that already makes sense.
